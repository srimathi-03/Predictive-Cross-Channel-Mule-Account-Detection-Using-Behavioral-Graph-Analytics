Problem

The Cross-Channel Mule Account Problem

  • Mule accounts move stolen funds across bank, wallet, UPI and card channels.
  • Traditional AML rules are siloed and focus on single transactions, not relationships.
  • Fraudsters rotate SIMs, devices, and accounts to stay invisible.
  • Banks face high loss exposure and reputational risk from late or missed mule detection.
Onboarding → Funds In → Rapid Cash Out → Detection (too late)
Gaps

Why Current Systems Fail

  • Brittle rule-based monitoring – small pattern changes evade detection.
  • Channel silos – card, UPI, internet banking and wallets are monitored separately.
  • No first-class graph view of devices, SIMs, merchants and IP relationships.
  • Slow investigations – analysts manually stitch data from multiple tools.

Result: mules are often detected weeks after onboarding, if at all.

Solution

Behavioral Graph Intelligence for Mule Detection

  • Unify cross-channel signals into a behavioral graph of accounts, devices, SIMs, IPs and merchants.
  • Continuously score every account with a Mule Intelligence Score (0–100).
  • Detect suspicious clusters and cash-out paths in near-real time.
  • Provide a SaaS dashboard for risk teams: KPIs, analysis tools, alerts and simulations.

From reactive case handling to predictive, graph-driven prevention.

Behavioral Graph

Behavioral Graph Analytics Concept

  • Nodes represent accounts, devices, SIMs, merchants, IPs and clusters.
  • Edges capture relationships: shared device / SIM, common IP, cash-out paths, co-merchant usage.
  • Graph features: degree centrality, shared neighbors, cluster density, temporal motifs.
  • These features feed into the Mule Intelligence Score and cluster-level alerts.
Visual: red mule hub node connected to multiple blue accounts and green devices; suspicious cluster highlighted for analysts.
Risk Model

Mule Intelligence Scoring

  • Score range 0–100 combining weighted behavioral and graph signals.
  • Features (examples):
    • SIM change frequency (last 30 days)
    • Recent account openings (90 days)
    • Cashout timing score vs. inflows
    • Night transaction ratio
    • Shared device / SIM count with flagged accounts
    • Cluster risk based on high-risk neighbors
  • Output: Score + Risk Category (Low / Medium / High / Severe) + Top drivers for explainability.

Example: Score 82 = Severe with drivers “shared high-risk device”, “night cashouts”, “SIM swap”.

Simulation

Simulation & Prediction Module

  • Projects fraud exposure over 30 / 60 / 90 days given current trends.
  • Inputs: base exposure (M USD), daily growth %, mitigation effectiveness %.
  • Outputs: raw vs. mitigated exposure curves for each horizon.
  • Supports “what if we improve detection by X%?” conversations with risk committees and boards.
Visual: three small line charts – red line = no controls, green line = with controls, showing widening gap (loss avoided).
Impact

Impact & Benefits for Banks

  • Earlier mule detection reduces direct fraud losses and write-offs.
  • Lower false positives by combining behavior + graph context rather than single rules.
  • Faster investigations with network visualization and explainable scores.
  • Improved regulatory alignment with holistic, risk-based AML monitoring.
  • SaaS delivery enables multi-entity roll-out across regions and subsidiaries.

Illustrative: 30–40% lower mule-related exposure, 50% faster investigations.
